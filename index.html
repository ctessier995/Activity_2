Overview

The NHL has a storied history starting in 1917. The league began with only 5 Canadian teams and has grown to 31 teams across the country today with a 32nd team to be added in the next few years. The NHL is seen as the premier league around the world, attracting talent from many different countries, and is still continuing to try and grow their international brand. The first U.S. team was the Boston Bruins, added in 1924. During the time period between the founding in 1917, league membership grew to 10 members but then dropped back down to 6 after 1942. These six teams are referred to as the Original Six. There was no further growth or loss until 1967, when the NHL added a professional sports record of six new teams all based in the United States. From this first wave of expansion until the NHL merged with another league, the WHA (World Hockey Association), in 1979. During this "era", there were new teams being added consistently every few years until 2000 when the league reached 30 teams.

NHL Expansion Today

The NHL has come back to expansion in recent years, expanding to Las Vegas in 2018 and just granting a franchise to Seattle that will enter the league in 2021. With this new growth, the NHL is hoping to break into new markets to continue the growth of the game. To assist in the growth of the international game, the NHL has begun to take their teams to other countries to play games. This season, the Calgary Flames and Boston Bruins played two pre-season games in China, the Edmonton Oilers and New Jersey Devils played in Sweden to begin the season, and the Winnepeg Jets and Florida Panthers played two games in Finland. This has all been in an effort to hopefully move the game beyond the North American market. Since the NHL is the smallest of the four major North American leagues, those being the NFL, NBA, MLB, and NHL, the league is trying to be forward thinking in how they grow in order to try and maintain growth while not running into issues with the other leagues.
